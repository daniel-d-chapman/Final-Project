Exploring Melbourne

Melbourne is a smorgasbord of different cultures! Home to the world's oldest China Town, Little Italy on Lygon street in the cities North, and the world's largest Greek population anywhere outside of Greece!

It is a city that boasts a thriving cafe culture, world class eateries and a vibrant art scene. It is totally obsessed with sports, and can lay claim as the Sports capital of the World. Melburnians even enjoy two Public Holidays every year didicated solely to large sporting events, not including other annual sporting events that fall on existing Public Holidays such as Good Friday, the King's Birthday or ANZAC Day!

Here is our list of the best places to visit and explore while visiting Melbourne

Melbourne Laneways

Melbourne's various Laneways are always popular with locals and tourist alike. Home to numerous quirky hidden bars, resturants and cafes, they are a favourite spot for anyone looking for an excelent dining experience, or a late night cocktail. They are also famous for their street art with the graffiti adorned laneways paying tribute to both musical heroes and local sporting stars. Must see spots include ACDC Lane, Degraves Street, Hardware Lane and Tattersall Lane.

St. Kilda

St. Kilda is a bayside suburb located 30 minutes east of Melbourne's CBD via tram. It is known for it's live music scene, and is a popular spot for local artist. Located by the beach it features an array of outdoor activities that include St. Kilda Music and Film Festivals, and Pride March. The suburb's local Theme Park Luna Park has become an enduring symbol of the beach side suburb.

Richmond

Richmonds sits just east of Melbourne's CBD and can easily be reached via tram or even walking if the weather is nice. It is the cities sporting district, and is home to the M.C.G. the home of Austrlian Rules Football in Winter and cricket in Summer. Melbourne Park is also located just across the road and plays home to the annual first tennis Grand Slam each year, the Australian Open. Richmond is also home to many popular pubs and eateries, and has a thriving local scene of musicians and artist.

Fitzroy

Fitroy and its neighbouring Collingwood are lively inner city suburbs known for their bohemian reputation, with an eclectic mix of bars, pubs, cafes and eateries. It is a popular destination for students, young professionals and backpackers. It has a vibrant art scene and is home to many local, talented creative types. It also is home to Edinburgh Gardens, one of Melbourne's largest public parks. It is ever popular among young and old alike, and is perhaps infamous as a popular hangout for locals enjoying some tasty alcoholic beverages while enyoying the sometimes fickle Melbourne weather.

Carlton

Carlton is home to Carlton Gardens where Australia's first national Parliament house was located at the Royal Exhibition Building. The parkland and beautiful building are still popular among locals and host numerous yearly events. Carlton is also famous for its Italian district of Lygon Street, famous for its resturants, and eateries, and the local cinema Nova where locals can watch a flick before checking out the Readings bookshop across the road.

Queen Victoria Market

Located in Melbourne's CBD, the Queen Victoria Market is the largest open air market in the Southern Hemisphere. A great place to buy local produce, and jag a bargain, especially if you arrive just before closing time when many tradings are selling fresh local produce at a heavily discounted rate. The market is also popular for hosting it's seasonal night markets where people can enjoy numerous food stalls and options and live music over cocktails.

South Wharf

Once a black sheep of the Melbourne scene, South Wharf has shed it's previous reputation as a quiet, empty pocket of Melbourne's CBD. It is now home to numerous excellent lakeside bars, eateries and pubs. It even boast a large German style drinking hall where locals and tourist can celebrate Oktoberfest each year. South Wharf is also popular with shoppers hitting up the D.F.O. looking for bargains and all sorts of fashionable items.

Royal Botanical Gardens

Located just a short walk from Flinders Street Station in the heart of Melbourne CBD, the Royal Botanical Gardens welcome almost 2 million visters annually. Covering 38 hectares and located next to the Yarra River, it is a wonderful spot to walk around, or just sitting down to relax while admiring the beautiful flora of the gardens.
